Clinical summary: This meta-analysis of patients with severe sepsis or septic shock suggests that the clinical intervention is effective if appropriate antibiotics are administered within 1 hour. Not able to be pooled due to methof of rporting benefit, the large Kaiser cohort (Liu, 2017) also supports this intervention, including in the subgroup of patients iwth severe sepsis. The heterogeneity in stuides of timing was 'may not be important' (residual I2 = 18%). We included cohort studies of treating patients with severe sepsis or septic shock with adequate antibiotics that reported the benefit of administraton within one hour compared to more than one hour. We exluded studies that did not report the benefit adjusted for severity of illness. Additional interpretations may be in the references in the 'Cited by' section below.

Clinical summary: This meta-analysis of patients with severe sepsis or septic shock suggests that the clinical intervention is effective. Studies of timing of antibiotics show benefit if the analysis adjusted for severity of illness as sicker patients are likely to receive antibiotics faster. Studies of choice of antibiotics show benefit if if all patient received recommended antibiotics. The heterogeneity in stuides of timing was 'substantial' (residual I2 = 74%). This may represent variation in choice of antibiotics. The quality of evidence supporting benefit is very low due to reliance on observational studies. Not able to be included in the meta-analysis are 6 cohorts that reported adjusted mortality rates or odds over time (Kumar, 2006 PMID 16625125; Ferrer, 2014 PMID 24717459; Pruinelli, 2017 PMID 29298189; Liu, 2017 PMID 28345952; Peltan, 2019 PMID 30779916; Kashiouris, 2019 PMID 31343474) that graphically display increasingly adjusted mortality with delays in antibiotic administration. We included cohort studies of treating patients with antibiotics that reported the benefit of administraton within one hour compared to more than one hour. Additional interpretations may be in the references in the 'Cited by' section below.
